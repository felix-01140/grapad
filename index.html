Who we are

We help people rebuild — not from scratch, but from where they are.

GRAPAD asbl is a community-based organisation officially registered in the DRC, based in Bukavu, South Kivu. We specialise in the social reintegration of populations affected by armed conflict — children pulled from the streets, women rebuilding after loss, families displaced by war finding their way back to a normal life.

Our motto says it plainly: “Universal well-being means living for others.” We do not work for these communities from the outside. We work with them — walking alongside the people war has disrupted, until they can walk on their own again.

Our direction

Vision, mission and values

A peaceful Africa where every person can fully realise their social, cultural and economic potential free from war and discrimination.

Where we work

Bukavu and Walungu, South Kivu, DRC with a broader presence across the Great Lakes region including Rwanda, Burundi and Uganda.

Our mission

To enlighten public opinion, promote peace, protect human rights and support the most vulnerable particularly women and children affected by armed conflict.

Our values

Transparency, teamwork, compassion, equality. We are apolitical and non-denominational committed only to the dignity of every person.

Eastern DRC

The reality on the ground

In eastern DRC, the consequences of decades of armed conflict are visible every day. These are the realities that drive our work.

Women and children bear the heaviest burden of the recurring wars in eastern DRC, often left with nothing when conflict strikes their families.

South Kivu hosts over 2.4 million internally displaced people. Many have received no assistance since their arrival in overcrowded sites.

Communities in South Kivu are rebuilding one family, one child, one act of solidarity at a time. GRAPAD walks alongside them.

Goma, North Kivu, March 2025

Humanitarian Support for Displaced Women and Families

In March 2025, the GRAPAD team travelled to Goma to support 62 displaced women and their families affected by the ongoing crisis in North Kivu. The team distributed essential kits and spent time listening, accompanying and documenting the situations of the most vulnerable. Every person reached was a step toward dignity restored.

🎓 Child Protection
Walungu, South Kivu — 15 May 2026

Field Mission Walungu, Mupe Nafasi Shuleni

On 15 May 2026, the GRAPAD team went to Walungu. Not for a photo. To look these children in the eyes, hand them a kit, and tell them: you matter. You are not forgotten. 80 pupils across 4 schools were reached, and 20 new children identified for the next phase of reintegration.

“Abandoned children become adults in difficulty and the country falls behind. Let us learn to feed them, educate them, protect them and listen to them.”
